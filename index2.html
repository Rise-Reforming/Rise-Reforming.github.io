A Tale
of Two Problems:

Plastic Waste and Fossil-Based Chemical Emissions

Only 5% of the 50 million tons of plastic waste generated in the US annually is recycled. In landfills, 40% decomposes in 25 years, but the other 60% stays around for thousands.

The fossil-based chemical industry still accounts for 5% of greenhouse gass emissions and must be decarbonized by 2050 in order to meet the United Nation's net zero carbon emissions goals.
